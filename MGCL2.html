Magnesium chloride is mainly used in three places, cuisine, health and ice control. In cuisine, it is used as a coagulant in tofu, this is important as if the tofu does not coagulate, then it would stay as a liquid. In the medical industry, it is used as a magnesium supplement and is used in baby formula. Magnesium chloride is used in ice control to make the ice not stick to the road and helps the ice melt. This is important because it makes the snowplough more effective in cleaning the road. Magnesium chloride is hazardous in large amounts as in the environment it will pollute it in large quantities. Normally in a body, excess magnesium chloride is excreted in the urine, but in excess amounts, it can cause mild sickness. Overall magnesium chloride is a helpful compound that is used around the world in many different sectors of the economy.
